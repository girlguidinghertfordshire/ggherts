There are many ways to say thank you, and to acknowledge all that is done within guiding, including [County](#county), [Region](#region) and [National](#national) awards.

## County Awards
{:#county}

![County Award Dinner](/wp-content/uploads/2019/05/p1140471_46955040804_o-300x225.jpg){:.pull-right.m-2}{:width="252"}{:height="189"}These recognise and reward the work that our volunteers do which is *over and above* that expected in the recipients Guiding role. These Awards are **not** for length of service - *service awards* are automatically presented to members for years of service in Guiding to recognise the service and dedication of volunteers in their role.

We are looking forward to the upcoming **Garden Party Awards Event** in June, celebrating this year's award recipients' achievements, with their friends and VIPs.

### Nominations for County Awards

Nominations for county awards can be made at any time throughout the year. Details of the three county awards are below along with guidance on the criteria for each:

- [Hertfordshire County Thanks Certificate](#hertfordshire-county-thanks-certificate)
- [Hertfordshire County Good Service Certificate](#hertfordshire-county-good-service-certificate)
- [Hertfordshire Good Guiding Badge](#hertfordshire-good-guiding-badge)

Before making an award nomination, please read the specific criteria for the award (below) and ensure:

- Each nominee should be proposed and seconded by adult members of Girlguiding Hertfordshire
- Neither of these should be related to the nominee nor each other
- The proposer completes a nomination form
- The proposer writes a letter supporting the nomination
- The seconder also writes a letter supporting the nomination but with, if possible, a different slant on the person being nominated
- The nomination form should approved by the appropriate commissioner who must not be either the proposer or seconder and not related to any party.
- The commissioner signs the form and attachs the Guiding Record

All award applications are confidential so it is important that neither nominee nor any family member/s are aware of the application.

### Hertfordshire County Thanks Certificate

**This Certificate can be awarded to unit helpers, treasurers, administrators, etc.**

This certificate can be awarded to an adult who has been nominated by a leader or their district, division, or county, and who is recognised by the proposers as having given excellent service to Girlguiding Hertfordshire.

The nominee, who need not be a member of Guiding, should demonstrate their support for the principles of Girlguiding.

A nominee may have been the recipient of a *Thanks Certificate* in a previous role.

### Hertfordshire County Good Service Certificate

This certificate is awarded to an **Adult Member** who has been nominated by a leader or their district, division, or county, and who is recognised by the proposers as having given excellent service to Girlguiding Hertfordshire. This certificate is **not awarded automatically** to adults completing a term of office

An adult being nominated for the County Good Service Certificate should be:

- recognised as trying to live by the standards of the Promise and the Law and the 5 essentials.
- demonstrate outstanding interpersonal skills appropriate to their role and a willingness to take a full and active part in the District/Division/County teams as appropriate to their role.
- recognised by adults, parents and young members (as appropriate to their role) as being a role model for Girlguiding.

Whilst this award is **not given to recognise service**, it is anticipated that the majority of recipients will have been delivering great guiding experiences for a sustained period of time.

A nominee may have been the recipient of a *Good Service Certificate* in a previous role.

### Hertfordshire Good Guiding Badge

**This badge can be awarded to an Adult Member.**

![A Hertfordshire Good Guiding Badge](/wp-content/uploads/2017/06/Goodguidingbadge-300x155.jpg){:.pull-left.m-2}{:width="223"}{:height="115"} This badge is awarded in recognition of continually delivering **exceptionally ‘good guiding’ experiences** to members of Girlguiding Hertfordshire.

The nominees should demonstrate their enthusiasm and ability in providing ongoing quality and challenging experiences to their unit, section, district, division or county in line with the Promise and Law and the 5 essentials.

A nominee should be recognised by adults, parents and young members (as appropriate to their role) as being a role model for Girlguiding.

Whilst this award is **not given to recognise service**, it is anticipated that the majority of recipients will have been delivering great guiding experiences for a sustained period of time.

In certain circumstances this award may be given to recognise exceptional contribution to an event providing other criteria have been met.

A nominee may have been a recipient of a *County Good Service Certificate* or *County Thanks Certificate* in the past

## Girlguiding Anglia Awards
{:#region}

For more information about Anglia Awards and details on how to make a nomination visit the [Girlguiding Anglia website](https://www.girlguiding-anglia.org.uk/resource/thanks-and-recognition-overview){:.external-link}{:target="_blank"}{:rel="noopener"}

### Anglia Brooch & Certificate

![Anglia Brooch](/wp-content/uploads/2017/06/Angliabrooch-150x150.jpg){:.pull-right.m-2}{:width="150"}{:height="150"}
This Girlguiding Anglia award is for **exceptional quality of service** given (not just length) and is for service within the region, not to the region. It is awarded for:

1. Commitment and level of service that has been over and above ‘the call of duty’ in respect of one or more appointments/events within or to guiding, or The Trefoil Guild, or;
2. A notable display of mental or physical courage in pain or adversity or;
3. A notable display of bravery or;
4. A demonstration of initiative and devotion to duty (without risk to their own life).

It is suggested that a minimum of five supporting letters are included with nominations.

[Anglia Brooch Nomination Form](https://www.girlguiding-anglia.org.uk/resource/anglia-brooch-nomination-form){:.btn.btn-primary.external-link}{:target="_blank"}{:rel="noopener"}

### Anglia Youth Award & Certificate

This can be awarded to a member of Girlguiding Anglia who is under the age of 18.  This Award is made for demonstration of at least one of the following elements:

1. A notable commitment and level of participation which has been over and above the ‘norm’ within a unit, district, division, county or in the region or;
2. A notable demonstration of initiative and adherence to the Guide Laws or;
3. A notable difference to the lives of others through supporting a Charity or worthy cause, other than Girlguiding, over a number of years or through a ‘one off’ large event or;
4. A notable demonstration of speaking out or empowering others where they wouldn’t normally have a voice, to influence decision makers or;
5. A notable demonstration of how they have actively championed the rights of girls and/or young women.

It is suggested that a minimum of five supporting letters are included with nominations.

[Anglia Youth Award Nomination Form](https://www.girlguiding-anglia.org.uk/resource/anglia-youth-award-nomination-form){:.btn.btn-primary.external-link}{:target="_blank"}{:rel="noopener"}

### Anglia Buddy Award

#### Nominating an Individual

Recognising the work of girls and young women who act as Buddies to members with special needs within units and at events. To nominate someone for the award they need to be aged 25 and under and a member of Girlguiding Anglia, and have worked as a Buddy to support a member or members with special needs for at least 25 hours. This must be on at least three separate occasions. The nomination should be made by a unit leader and, where possible, supported in some way by the young member(s) with whom they work.

#### Nominating a Unit

Recognising the units which contain girls and young women who regularly help and support members with special needs in those units each week during their meetings. To nominate a unit for the Girlguiding Anglia Buddy award they must be within Girlguiding Anglia and have supported a member or members with special needs for at least 25 hours. This must be on at least three separate occasions. The nomination should be made by a district, division or county commissioner or Chair of county awards Committee and, where possible, supported in some way by the young member(s) with whom they work.

[Anglia Buddy Award Nomination Form](https://www.girlguiding-anglia.org.uk/resource/anglia-buddy-award-nomination-form){:.btn.btn-primary.external-link}{:target="_blank"}{:rel="noopener"}

## Girlguiding Awards
{:#national}

![Girlguiding UK Long Service Award](/wp-content/uploads/2017/06/service16-300x200.jpg){:.pull-right.m-2}{:width="300"}{:height="200"}
There are many national awards that can be given to volunteers. These include:

- Thanks Badge
- Good Service Brooch
- Long Service Awards (awarded automatically)

Members can also be nominated for the following discretionary awards:

- The Guiding Star
- The Laurel Award
- The Silver Fish
- National Honours (e.g. MBE, OBE, CBE)

[See more information about Girlguiding awards.](https://www.girlguiding.org.uk/making-guiding-happen/running-your-unit/managing-your-team/awards-and-recognition/){:.external-link}{:target="_blank"}{:rel="noopener"}
