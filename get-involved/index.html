There are many ways to get involved. Here you can find more information on each section, as well as how to register yourself or your daughter to join.

Girls and Young Women

Rainbows

Ages 4-7

Rainbows learn by doing — by following an engaging programme they get their hands dirty, do sports, arts and crafts and play games.

Being a Rainbow is all about having the space to try new things. Through taking part in a range of different activities with girls their own age they develop self-confidence and make lots of new friends.

They can work towards interest badges, where they can learn about everything from recycling to storytelling, and start their skills builder journey, developing core skills and trying new things like camping, leadership and first aid.

Brownies

Ages 7-10

Brownies is about getting together regularly - often each week - to work on badges, learn new skills, play games and see friends. It's about trying adventurous activities and going along to special events, day trips, sleepovers, camps and holidays.

All Brownies are sure to try new things and take on a broad range of challenges. They work together to explore the world around them and make a positive difference in their community.

Learn more about brownies

Guides

Ages 10-14

In Guides you'll get out there and do something really different! Go on adventures and try new things. As you progress through Guides you'll make friends, learn, and share new skills and may fly down the occasional zip wire.

You'll explore new places, go on camps, holidays and trips. Work towards badges and you can even start volunteering by becoming a Rainbow or Brownie helper.

Rangers

Ages 14-18

Rangers is a brilliant way to try new things, have fun and give something back to your community. Whether you're already a member, or are just starting out, we'll give you a host of opportunities to gain leadership skills, build a network of friends in your area and enhance your CV.

Flexible Guiding

In addition to the normal unit offerings we are pleased to inform you that Girlguiding Hertfordshire also has Flexible Guiding Units. This means we can offer all the benefits of being a Girlguiding member in a way that fits around your needs and schedule. For more details please visit the Flexible Guiding Units webpage.

The next step in her guiding journey

When your daughter becomes old enough, she'll be able to move up into the next section of Girlguiding.

Here's a guide to when it might happen:

  • Rainbows move up to Brownies aged 7
  • Brownies move up to Guides aged 10
  • Guides move up to Rangers aged 14

How to move your daughter on

In advance of your daughter's time to move on to the next section, speak with their current unit leader about available local opportunities. There might not be a place for them straight away, so they may have to be added to your chosen unit’s waiting list. It is therefore advisable to make enquiries as soon as possible.

Volunteers

Ages 18 plus

There are thousands of adult volunteers who help within Girlguiding in so many ways, such as:

  • Running a unit as a leader, assistant leader or helper
  • Helping with the accounts or administration
  • Teaching skills such as First Aid, or adventurous activities like sailing or climbing and units will often welcome support
  • Supporting one of the local Girlguiding volunteer shops and depots
  • Carry out gardening or DIY at a local site
  • Fundraising for trips and special events

Volunteering can be beneficial in so many ways. You can learn new skills, boost your CV, as well as making friendships that can last a lifetime. Volunteers are supported by local Commissioners, can attend training courses and work on qualifications to help develop their skills.

By volunteering with us you can support girls and young women to be their best as well as becoming part of the Girlguiding Inspire community (18-30).
